Teacher Page

Posted on 14 May by Joe Bloggs

Welcome to the WebQuest lesson plan on creativity. This page is designated to educators who wish to see in greater detail the purpose, objectives and learner characteristics associated to this WebQuest.

Purpose

The WebQuest was designed to help you to better understand and develop your understanding and application of creativity and the creative process. These series of tasks promote students to use and to build on a new set of skills in order to further push their imagination in a new direction, and get their creative juices flowing. Building from one idea, in this case challenge, to create another idea. To help the students learn a new skill, the online platform Kids Learning Skills and Being Awesome is used. This website is already set up to inspire children 16 and under to try out a new skill and accumulate points to earn a badge by completing challenges.

Rationale

For nearly two decades, a downfall in creative abilities is more apparent than ever in today’s adults and students. Research papers such as, The Creativity Crisis: The Decrease in Creative Thinking Scores on the Torrance Tests of Creative Thinking by Dr. Kyung Hee Kim, a Professor at The College of William and Mary, explains in more detail this phenomenon. Since the 1990, a shift towards test-taking skills became a priority for the American Politiciens. The political push occurred do to a fear to stay on top of academic educational and economic success seen in other neighboring Asian Countries.

Reintroducing the creative process in the classroom is more important now than ever. It is part of the top skills required in today's workforce. It is also present in our educational standards linked to innovation. We are born will the natural abilities to be creative, but as many other skills, this needs to be practiced as it too can become obsolete if not used and developed. Targeting specific training, the creative area is a need not only for today’s student but also for today’s teacher.

Learner Description

This WebQuest was designed for students in Grade 5-9, aged 12 to 16. This is due to the age requirement for using the Kids Learning Skills and Being Awesome website (www.diy.org). Of course, parental permission can allow for younger students to participate at their discretion.

Prerequisites

It is assumed that students who will be utilizing this WebQuest are familiar with basic Web browser navigation skills. Flipgrid and using the Kids Learning Skills and Being Awesome website does not need to be known prior to this WebQuest. These tools have been picked to represent the 21st century needs of today’s digital student.

Instructional Objectives

As a result of completing this WebQuest the learner will be able to:

  1. describe and define creativity.
  2. explain and give examples of creativity.
  3. practice and develop new skills to stimulate their creativity.
  4. discuss and analyze creative challenges and reasoning.
  5. complete and share an original creative challenge.

Standards Addressed

The set of standards selected for this WebQuest are based on the Alberta Information and Communication Technology curriculum. The specific standard met through this WebQuest are based that technology is about the ways things are done; the processes, tools and techniques that alter human activity. ICT is about the new ways in which we can communicate, inquire, make decisions and solve problems.

It is the processes, tools and techniques for:

  • gathering and identifying information
  • classifying and organizing
  • summarizing and synthesizing
  • analyzing and evaluating
  • speculating and predicting

The ICT curriculum presents these concepts within three interrelated categories:

  • communicating, inquiring, decision making and problem solving
  • foundational operations, knowledge and concepts
  • processes for productivity

This WebQuest on the creative process experience responds to the ICT outcomes.

Subject Matter Description

This WebQuest follows the model developed by Bernie Dodge and Tom March. It contains the following sections for learners:

Introduction: This section provides an overview to help learners to become familiar with the topic.

Task: The task to be completed is described in this section. Learners will develop a policy brief outlining blogger rights and liabilities. This document will be prepared in a world processor. The final version should be high quality and suitable for informational use in a school or workplace.

Process: The list of steps to follow are provided. Learners will synthesize information found in a series of articles and videos. A discussion forum will be used to talk about the problems, issues, and consequences of blogging discovered in the articles and videos. The discussion is held online for one week. After the discussion week learners will work individually to write a policy brief that is suitable for their own school or workplace.

Evaluation: A rubric is provided to allow learners to self-score their projects. This same rubric will be used by the instructor to assign a grade for work completed in this WebQuest.

Conclusion: This section is used to prompt the learner to think about what they have learned during this WebQuest. What are the implications for the future?

Assessment

Students will create an original creative project based on a new skill developed throughout this WebQuest process. The end result will be assessed using the Creative Project Rubric located in the Evaluation section of the WebQuest.
