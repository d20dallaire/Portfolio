Web Accessibility for the Visually-Impaired and Blind

The World Wide Web allows access to an unimaginable amount of information through a variety of web resources. These sources should be equally accessible to people with a variety of physical and cognitive abilities. Web Accessibility focuses on removing barriers of communication between the web and its users. This allows everyone to access, view, interact, navigate and, more importantly, contribute to the Web.

Imagine yourself trying to navigate through the World Wide Web with your eyes closed. According to the National Federation of the Blind, it is "estimated that as many as 10 million Americans are blind or visually-impaired" and that "each year 75,000 more people become blind or visually-impaired". The Web Accessibility Initiative (WAI) has made it their mission to lead the Web to its full potential and ensure that people with disabilities have the same opportunities to actively participate on the Web. Without specialized software or applications, this task would be nearly impossible for these individuals. A valuable tool that can assist these individuals to this end is a screen reader.

Screen Readers are software programs or applications that can read the text on a webpage aloud to increase web accessibility. Furthermore, many of these assistive softwares can read or spell out individual words as well as tell the user which keyboard keys have been pressed. For more information, visit the American Foundation for the Blind's description or view this Introduction to Screen Readers video .

Screen Readers: Essential Tools

Below is a list of the top free or paid screen reader software or applications available today.


Apple VoiceOver: Overview of the Apple VoiceOver tool, a free screen reader available on Mac devices.

NonVisual Desktop Access (NVDA): Overview of a the NVDA, a free screen reader available on Windows computers.

Serotek System Access: Greeted with a computerized voice, this link gives you access to a free screen reader available for download by clicking on the "Begin" link.

Job Access with Speech (JAWS): This link provides a description as well as the opportunity to purchase one of the most recommended screen reader softwares available for Windows computers.

WebAnywhere: Overview of a screen reader that can be used on any device, which allows the visually-impaired and people with blindness to use this tool wherever they go.

Google ChromeVox: Detailed video explaining this screen reader and providing step-by-step instructions for its use. This tool is only available on Google devices and Google Chrome.

How to Make an Accessible Website?

The following links provide tips and tricks on making a website that is accessible to the visually-impaired and people with blindness.


WebAIM Article: Explains considerations to be taken for people with blindness as well as screen readers and their limitations. This site can also link to more information regarding web accessibility for people with a variety of visual disabilities.

Stanford University Article: Provides an overview of a developer's responsibilities, the way screen readers work and the way to test your site for screen reader capabilities.

American Foundation for the Blind Article: Explains considerations that need to be taken for the technology used to view a website for the visually-impaired or people with blindness.
