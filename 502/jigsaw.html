Learner Description

This activity is designed to assist educators at any grade-level who will be introducing Minecraft in their classroom.

Introduction

The following jigsaw activity is a cooperative learning technique that allows for a big concept to be simplified into sub-concepts, mastered, and then shared with others. This jigsaw method of learning is based on the Elliot Aronson’s Jigsaw Classroom model.

Note: All links on this website will open in a new browser window.

Instructions

  1. The teacher prepares a Minecraft project and then presents it to the class. Here is a link to MinecraftEdu resources and lesson ideas.

  2. Have a general discussion on the subject of Minecraft with the students to gauge their knowledge going into this introductory activity.

  3. Divide students into 6 groups. (Note: Use your prior discussion to evenly separate those with existing expertise.)

  4. The students in each group will then be given a number from 1 to 6. Each number corresponds to a specific jigsaw group. They will become the experts of that sub-topic.

  5. All the students that were given a 1 group together and work on the jigsaw group question 1. This will continue with the groups 2, 3, 4, 5 and 6.

  6. Each number group will then need to answer the Minecraft Basics 101 question by exploring the linked resources provided in the table below.

  7. Students will then reunite with their initial groups and share their knowledge as the expert on their Minecraft Basics 101 question.
