Answer Key

Security Question 1: The act of plagiarizing something is defined by the Merriam-Webster as:

  • The act of using another person's words or ideas without giving credit to that person. Source

Security Question 2: The most important part of avoiding plagiarism:

  • Acknowledging the original source of another person's ideas or words Source

Security Question 3: Which of the following is considered plagiarism. Answer by selecting true or false.

  • Part 1: Changing words but copying the sentence structure of a source with link to the resource. FALSE
  • Part 2: Failing to put a quotation in quotation marks. TRUE
  • Part 3: Copying many words or ideas from a source that it makes up the majority of your work, whether you give credit or not. TRUE
    Source

Security Question 4: What are the top 5 different types of plagiarism according to the reThinkELA article?

  • clone, copy/paste, find/replace, remix, recycle Source

Security Question 5: Self-plagiarism is considered plagiarism when:

  • You are borrowing from your previous work or when you are not creating new original work. Source 1 Source 2

Security Question 6: What skills will you acquire if you learn to write in your own words?

  • Helps to communicate effectively, understand information, learn and retain knowledge, critical thinking, and builds good habits. Source

Security Question 7: When do you need to give credit for the information you are using?

  • When the words come from any external resource. Source

Security Question 8: What are the 5 simple rules you can apply when reusing online content?

  • Name the author, separate original and reused content, don't hotlink images, clear attribute, and do not "no follow" attribution links. Source

Security Question 9: When do you not need to cite your information?

  • When the information you present is common knowledge. Source 1 Source 2

Security Question 10: Which of the following links are online plagiarism checkers and detection software you can use to verify your work?

  • Plagiarism.org, WriteCheck, Quetext
